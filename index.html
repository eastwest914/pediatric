Frequently Asked Questions

5 FAQs — Pediatric Orthodontist | Kids Braces Orthodontist | Children Orthodontic Treatment

At what age should my child first see a Pediatric Orthodontist? +

Most orthodontic associations recommend a first evaluation around age 7, when early signs of bite or spacing issues can be identified even before all baby teeth have fallen out.

How long does children's orthodontic treatment typically take? +

Treatment length varies by case, but most kids braces programs run between 12 and 24 months, depending on the complexity of the alignment or bite correction needed.

Are braces painful for kids? +

Mild soreness for a few days after adjustments is normal, but our gentle techniques and clear aftercare guidance help keep discomfort minimal throughout treatment.

What's the difference between a general dentist and a Pediatric Orthodontist? +

A Pediatric Orthodontist has specialized training in guiding jaw growth and tooth movement in children, going beyond general dental care to focus on long-term alignment and bite health.

Do you offer flexible scheduling for busy families? +

Yes — we offer flexible appointment times designed to work around school and family schedules, making it easier to keep every visit on track.
